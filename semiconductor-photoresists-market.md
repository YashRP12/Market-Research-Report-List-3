<p><p>Semiconductor photoresists can be categorized into two main types - Negative Photoresists and Positive Photoresists. Negative photoresists are those that become more resistant to etching when exposed to light, while positive photoresists become less resistant. Negative photoresists are often preferred for their higher resolution and sharper images, while positive photoresists are known for their simpler processing and lower cost. Both types play a crucial role in the semiconductor industry for creating intricate patterns on semiconductor wafers during the manufacturing process.</p></p>
