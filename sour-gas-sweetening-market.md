<p><p>Sour gas sweetening refers to the process of removing harmful sulfur compounds from natural gas. Type I market for sour gas sweetening includes the use of physical solvents, while Type II market involves the use of chemical solvents. Physical solvents are typically more selective and have higher regeneration rates, making them more suitable for low sulfur content gases. Chemical solvents, on the other hand, are more effective at removing higher concentrations of sulfur compounds from gas streams. Both types of markets cater to different needs and preferences in the sour gas sweetening industry.</p></p>
