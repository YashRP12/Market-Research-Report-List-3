<p><p>High temperature lubricants and greases are categorized based on the type of thickener used in their formulation. Soap thickener greases are made using metallic soaps, which are derived from fatty acids and a metal hydroxide. Non-soap thickener greases, on the other hand, are produced using alternative thickeners such as polyurea, silica, PTFE, or clay. Each type of thickener has its own unique properties and benefits, making them suitable for different applications in high temperature environments.</p></p>
