<p><p>Contact sleep monitoring equipment typically requires physical contact with the user, such as wearing a device on the wrist or headband during sleep to monitor vital signs. Contactless sleep monitoring equipment, on the other hand, uses sensors that can detect vital signs without direct physical contact with the user, such as through radar or optical technology. Both types of equipment provide valuable data on sleep quality and patterns, allowing users to monitor and improve their overall health and well-being.</p></p>
