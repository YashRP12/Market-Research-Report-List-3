Freight transport brokerage plays a vital role in enhancing efficiency, ensuring cost savings, and improving overall supply chain management.</p><p>The global freight transport brokerage market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a robust CAGR of 9.1% during the forecast period. With the advent of advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, blockchain, and cloud computing, brokers are able to streamline their operations, automate processes, and provide real-time visibility to shippers and carriers. This trend not only enhances efficiency but also improves transparency and reduces operational costs.</p><p>Another trend is the growing popularity of on-demand and spot market services. <p><p>The freight transport brokerage market is divided into three main types: truckload, less than truckload (LTL), and other markets. The truckload market is focused on transporting large quantities of goods, where a full truck is dedicated to one shipment. On the other hand, the LTL market deals with smaller shipments that do not require a full truck, where several shipments from different customers are consolidated into one truck. The "other" market comprises specialized transportation services such as refrigerated goods, oversize loads, and expedited shipping.</p></p>
