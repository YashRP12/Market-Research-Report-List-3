<p><ul><li>Individual IP</li><li>Coimmunoprecipitation</li><li>CHIP</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Immunoprecipitation (IP) is a widely used technique in scientific research to study protein-protein interactions. It involves the precipitation of a specific protein of interest from a complex mixture using an antibody. Individual IP refers to isolating a single protein, whereas Coimmunoprecipitation (Co-IP) involves isolating multiple interacting proteins simultaneously. Co-IP helps understand protein complexes and their functions. Chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) identifies protein-DNA interactions, aiding in studying gene regulation. The immunoprecipitation market encompasses various tools and reagents used in these techniques, facilitating research on protein-protein and protein-DNA interactions.</p></p>
