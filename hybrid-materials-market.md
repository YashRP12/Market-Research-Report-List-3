<p><p>Hybrid materials in the market primarily fall into two categories: organic materials and inorganic materials. Organic materials are derived from carbon-based compounds and exhibit properties like flexibility, lightweight, and biodegradability. In contrast, inorganic materials are made up of non-carbon based elements like metals, ceramics, and glasses, providing characteristics such as strength, durability, and heat resistance. Both types of hybrid materials are widely used in various industries, including automotive, electronics, healthcare, and construction, due to their unique properties and versatility.</p></p>
